Review

 

Despite the global turmoil triggered by the war in Ukraine, the Indian economy demonstrated remarkable resilience in 2022-23 and achieved a growth rate of 7.0 percent, surpassing other major economies worldwide. Apart from a brief scare caused by the Omicron variant early in 2022, the impact of COVID-19 remained subdued for most of the year, leading to the restoration of consumer and business confidence.

The Indian economy achieved a significant milestone as the real GDP in level terms exceeded the pre-pandemic level of 2019-20 by 9.9 percent. Revised GDP growth figures for the preceding three years showed upward revisions of 20 bps (2019-20), 80 bps (2020-21), and 40 bps (2021-22), indicating that the growth momentum was stronger than initially estimated. Both consumption and investment demand gained traction, contributing to the growth. However, adverse external demand conditions and a larger deficit in net exports weighed on the overall growth in 2022-23. The economy maintained robust growth in H1:2022-23, with a notable acceleration in momentum from Q1 to Q2. However, the momentum moderated in Q3:2022-23.

Following a remarkable recovery post-COVID, India's exports experienced a slowdown, with growth easing to 11.5 percent in 2022-23 from a notable 29.3 percent in the previous year (2021-22). Meanwhile, imports grew at a faster pace of 18.8 percent, resulting in a widening trade deficit. As a result, the drag from net exports expanded to 2.9 percent of GDP in 2022-23, compared to 1.3 percent in the previous year.
